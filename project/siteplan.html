Site Plan — Winsley Jeune

Site Name

Proposed: Winsley Jeune — Web Systems & AI

Why this name? It’s direct, searchable, and accurately positions me as a developer who builds web systems and integrates AI. It keeps my personal name front-and-center for credibility and discoverability.

Domain: winsleyjeune.com, github.com/jeune07/w231/winsleydev

Site Purpose

This website serves as my digital portfolio and professional hub, designed to:

  • Demonstrate expertise through real-world projects with quantifiable results, detailed case studies, and technical breakdowns.
  • Attract clients & opportunities by clearly presenting services (Web Development, AI Integration, E-commerce Automation, Custom Systems) with transparent pricing and processes.
  • Share knowledge via Winsley's Lab — a technical blog covering algorithms, AI experiments, tutorials, and build notes that establish thought leadership.
  • Build trust through authentic storytelling, testimonials, open-source contributions, and a transparent approach to problem-solving.
  • Enable connection with multiple touchpoints: direct contact form, consultation booking, social proof, and easy-to-find contact information.

Core Goal: Convert visitors into clients, collaborators, or followers by demonstrating both technical skill and business impact.

Target Audience & User Scenarios

Primary Audiences

  • Potential Clients — Small to mid-size businesses, startups, and entrepreneurs seeking custom web solutions, AI integrations, or e-commerce automation.
  • Technical Recruiters & Hiring Managers — Companies looking for full-stack developers with AI/ML capabilities, seeking proof of technical depth and business impact.
  • Developer Community — Peers interested in learning from technical content, algorithms, and real-world implementation patterns.
  • Collaborators & Partners — Other developers, agencies, or technical leads looking for project partnerships or specialized expertise.

Key Questions Each Audience Asks

Clients ask:

  • "Can Winsley solve my specific problem (e-commerce, automation, AI)?"
  • "What results has he delivered for businesses like mine?"
  • "How much does it cost and what's the process?"
  • "Is he reliable, professional, and easy to work with?"

Recruiters ask:

  • "Does he have the technical skills we need (React, Node, AI, databases)?"
  • "Can I see proof of complex projects he's built from scratch?"
  • "Does he understand business requirements, not just code?"
  • "Is he actively learning and staying current with tech trends?"

Developers ask:

  • "What unique insights or techniques can I learn from his work?"
  • "Does he contribute to open source or share code examples?"
  • "How does he approach complex algorithmic or AI problems?"
  • "Is he active in the developer community?"

CSS & Component Notes

This plan document itself uses the selected color scheme and typography. Production components will follow a mobile‑first approach with utility classes and progressive enhancement. Cards, buttons, and navigation maintain contrast ratios for accessibility.

Key components: Header/Nav, Hero, Highlight cards, Project grid, Lab index, CTA, Footer.

Testing Plan

  • HTML/CSS Validation: Validate markup and CSS with standard validators.
  • Accessibility: Use browser DevTools / Lighthouse (mobile & desktop). Check focus states, landmarks, alt text.
  • Color Contrast: DevTools CSS Overview to ensure AA contrast for text/buttons.
  • Performance: Lighthouse performance (optimize images, font loading, avoid layout shift).
  • SEO: Descriptive titles/descriptions, semantic headings, sitemap/robots once deployed.

This site plan is a living document and may be refined as the build progresses.
